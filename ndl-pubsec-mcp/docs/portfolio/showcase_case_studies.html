Case studies

Three journeys. Three domains. Three people the system currently fails.

These are representative scenarios grounded in domain scoping research, not invented. Each is designed to show both the current failure and what a working tool changes.

MJ
Marjorie, 71
Retired · Manchester · Energy domain
Elderly Pension Credit Digital-assisted

Marjorie discovers she qualifies for the Warm Home Discount — automatically, no application needed

Marjorie's daughter notices she's wearing three jumpers inside in January and calls to check in. Marjorie says she's "managing fine" but admits she's been keeping the heating off. She's been on Pension Credit for three years. Neither she nor her daughter knows that Pension Credit Core Group entitles her to an automatic £150 Warm Home Discount every year — no application, it should just arrive. It hasn't. She's owed three years of it.
1

Marjorie's daughter uses the tool on her phone

She types: "My mum is on Pension Credit and is really struggling with heating bills. What help is available?" The tool is mobile-first, no login required, and responds in seconds.

energy_explain_scheme · scheme: "Warm Home Discount" · reading_level: "simple"
Returns: plain English explanation, £150 annual amount, eligibility (Pension Credit Core Group = automatic), how to check if it's been applied, supplier escalation route if not received.
2

She checks whether Marjorie has actually received it

The tool explains how to check with the supplier and what to say if it hasn't been applied. It confirms that Pension Credit Core Group should be automatic and provides the Ofgem complaint route if the supplier hasn't applied it.

energy_check_rights · situation: "benefit_not_received" · vulnerability_flags: ["elderly","pension_credit"]
Returns: Ofgem vulnerable customer obligations, supplier contact script, escalation path to Ofgem's Extra Help Unit if not resolved, plus Priority Services Register eligibility.
3

The supplier confirms the discount was never applied

Using the letter drafted by the tool and the Ofgem complaint reference, the family contacts the supplier. The discount is backdated and applied within ten days.

What changed

  • £150 annual discount applied and backdated where possible
  • Priority Services Register registration initiated — free additional support
  • ECO4 insulation assessment booked through energy_check_eligibility
  • Marjorie's daughter now knows where to check for all future energy entitlements
Blindspot this case surfaces: Marjorie's daughter is the actual tool user — not Marjorie. The proxy use pattern (a family member navigating on behalf of a vulnerable person) is one of the 15 structural blindspots identified in the UR bundle. The tool must be designed for this use case, not just self-advocacy.
RY
Ryan, 41
Newcastle · Legal domain
30-day deadline PIP appeal Crisis moment

Ryan's PIP appeal: a 30-day deadline he didn't know existed, caught with 16 days to spare

Ryan receives a PIP refusal letter on a Tuesday afternoon. He has fibromyalgia and depression. The letter says he scored 0 points on the functional assessment. He reads it once, puts it in a drawer, and tells himself he'll deal with it next week. He doesn't know there is a 30-day mandatory reconsideration deadline. He doesn't know that 74% of properly argued PIP appeals succeed at tribunal. By the time he thinks to look for help, it's Day 14.
1

Ryan's Citizens Advice adviser uses the tool during a walk-in appointment

She types: "PIP refused 14 days ago. What are the options and deadlines?" The tool immediately surfaces the time-critical context.

legal_check_deadlines · situation: "pip_refused" · trigger_date: "2026-05-28"
Returns: Mandatory Reconsideration deadline = 27 June 2026. Days remaining: 16. Urgency: HIGH. If missed: right of appeal closes permanently. Extension grounds: disability, mental health, or good reason. Call DWP immediately if you're in the extension window.
2

They understand what the assessment actually looked for — and why he scored zero

The tool explains PIP's functional descriptors in plain English, identifies which activities Ryan described during the appointment that score points, and shows what evidence the assessor should have seen.

ltc_prepare_evidence · assessment_type: "PIP" · conditions: ["fibromyalgia","depression"]
Returns: Which PIP descriptors apply to fluctuating conditions. What assessors look for on bad days vs. the average. How to describe pain on a 1–10 scale in the way the criteria require. What medical evidence to attach.
3

A properly structured Mandatory Reconsideration letter is submitted on Day 22

Using the tool's letter framework, citing the descriptors, with GP letter attached. The MR upholds the original decision — but the case is now structured correctly for tribunal. Ryan gets a welfare rights advocate and reaches tribunal 4 months later.

What changed

  • Deadline caught with 16 days to spare — appeal route preserved
  • MR letter structured around the correct legal criteria (not just "I disagree")
  • Tribunal reached — 74% of well-argued cases succeed
  • If successful: up to £9,584/year (Enhanced Daily Living + Enhanced Mobility PIP)
Blindspot this case surfaces: Ryan is in a crisis moment — not an information-seeking state. He put the letter in a drawer because crisis shuts down cognitive processing. The tool must be accessible to a stressed, overwhelmed person navigating on Day 14, not someone calmly researching options on Day 1.
KZ
Kezia, 27
Leeds (NDL pilot area) · Early years domain
Single parent Speech delay concern SEND unaware

Kezia navigates a speech delay concern from "I don't know what to do" to SALT referral in one session

Kezia's 3-year-old, Jaylen, has been flagged by his health visitor at his 2.5-year development check. She noted possible speech and language delay. Kezia was given a leaflet. Three months later, Kezia is more worried — Jaylen isn't putting words together the way other children his age are. She doesn't know what SALT means, what an EHCP is, or whether she can ask for a referral herself or if she needs a professional to do it.
1

Kezia uses the tool on her phone while Jaylen is napping

She types: "My 3-year-old has possible speech delay, health visitor flagged it 3 months ago, what should I do?" The tool responds with warmth and without alarm.

eyfs_check_development · child_age_months: 36 · concern_area: ["speech"] · user_type: "parent"
Returns: What's typical at 36 months for speech. What's worth getting checked. Reassuring note on range of typical development. Clear "when to seek help" guidance without diagnosing. Recommended next step: request a SALT referral — you can do this yourself. Disclaimer: this is not a diagnosis.
2

She finds out she can self-refer — she doesn't need the health visitor's permission

The tool shows that in most areas parents can self-refer to Speech and Language Therapy. It finds the referral pathway for Leeds specifically, with a direct phone number.

eyfs_find_support · postcode: "LS1 1AA" · support_type: ["speech_language"] · child_age_months: 36
Returns: Leeds SALT self-referral pathway (direct number, online form, waiting time estimate ~12 weeks). Leeds SEND local offer URL. Leeds Best Start Family Hub locations near her postcode. Cost: free. Self-referral: yes.
3

She understands what happens next and what her rights are if the wait is too long

The tool explains the difference between SEND Support (what the nursery should already be doing) and an EHCP (a legal document with statutory funding). She didn't know she could request an EHCP assessment herself.

What changed

  • SALT referral submitted — 3 months of unnecessary waiting ended
  • Jaylen's nursery now knows to put SEND Support in place while waiting
  • Kezia knows she can request an EHCP assessment independently if the wait exceeds statutory timeframes
  • Family Hub playgroup found — social connection for both Kezia and Jaylen
Research note: Leeds is one of the three NDL Early Years Kickstarter pilot areas (alongside Hammersmith & Fulham and Liverpool City Region). The local service data that makes step 2 possible is exactly the kind of connected data the NDL kickstarter is working to create. The tool works now with web search; it will work better with NDL data linkage.

Example output

What you actually see when you use the tools

This is a representative output from energy_explain_scheme — the first tool being built. Real tool. Real GOV.UK source. Plain English quality gate: FK Grade 5.4.

Tool call: energy_explain_scheme · scheme_name: "Warm Home Discount" · reading_level: "simple" · language: "en"
Scheme name
Warm Home Discount
What it is
A discount on your electricity or gas bill. You do not apply for it — if you qualify, your energy supplier should add it to your bill for you.
How much
£150 off your electricity or gas bill, once per year.
Who qualifies
If you get the Guarantee Credit part of Pension Credit, you should get it automatically. This is called the Core Group. Your supplier will contact you. Some suppliers also have a Broader Group for people on other benefits on low incomes — you may need to apply to your supplier directly for this.
Is an application needed?
For the Core Group: no. It should happen automatically. If it has not, contact your supplier and then Ofgem if the supplier does not help.
Steps if you have not received it
1. Call your energy supplier and quote "Warm Home Discount Core Group"
2. Ask them to check your account against DWP records
3. If they refuse, contact Ofgem's Extra Help Unit on 0808 223 1133
4. You may be owed backdated payment — ask for this specifically
Deadline
The scheme runs each winter. Suppliers must apply Core Group discounts by 31 March each year.
Source: GOV.UK Content API — /the-warm-home-discount-scheme — retrieved June 2026. Always verify current rates and eligibility at gov.uk/the-warm-home-discount-scheme
FK Grade 5.4 Source cited is_automatic: true Human route included

The same tool output can be requested in Welsh, Urdu, Somali, Polish, Bengali, Punjabi or Arabic — using the same source, with a standardised government terminology glossary applied. Multilingual layer is in Phase 2 of the build.
