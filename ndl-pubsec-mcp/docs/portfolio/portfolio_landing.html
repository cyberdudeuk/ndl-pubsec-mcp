The problem in numbers

The support exists. People just can't find it.

Across six domains, the same structural failure repeats: services built around departments rather than people, information locked in silos, and the most vulnerable citizens least able to navigate the gap.

Energy
4.5m

Fuel-poor households in England. ~10,000 cold-related deaths annually. Billions in support going unclaimed.

Long-term conditions
15m

People with LTCs repeatedly prove the same condition to services that can't share data. PIP and ESA take-up gaps run to billions.

Social care
400k+

People waiting for care needs assessments. NHS Continuing Healthcare — worth £40-60k per year — systematically unclaimed.

Legal
37%

Of legal problems go unresolved. 74% of benefit tribunal appeals succeed when properly argued. Most people give up before they get there.

Climate
5.2m

Properties at flood risk in England. Most haven't signed up for free EA alerts. Flood Re and adaptation grants largely unknown.

Early years
48%

Of FSM-eligible children start school without basic skills (32% nationally). Health visiting, education, and childcare operate in isolation.

How we're building it

Open, responsible, co-designed

The platform is built on a shared foundation of ten reusable modules, a TypeScript MCP server architecture, and a DEIA compliance layer that treats accessibility and plain language as technical invariants rather than optional features.

⚙
Model Context Protocol (MCP)
The industry-standard interface between AI assistants and external tools and data. Our 36 domain tools are MCP servers in TypeScript, built on @modelcontextprotocol/sdk. Compatible with any MCP-supporting AI assistant including GOV.UK Chat.
🇬🇧
Built on UK government public APIs
GOV.UK Content and Search APIs. EA Flood Risk API. CQC Provider API. legislation.gov.uk API. postcodes.io. All free, all public, all authoritative. Every tool output traces to a citable government source.
♿
DEIA compliance as a platform invariant
Flesch-Kincaid Grade ≤6 as a quality gate. WCAG 2.2 AA from the first build. Seven languages including Welsh (statutory). No login for guidance. Always a route to a human. These are not features — they are requirements that block deployment if unmet.
👥
Co-designed with communities
Not about communities — with them. Priority co-design partners: Disability Rights UK, Law Centres Network, Citizens Advice, SEND parent-carer forums, community flood groups, and energy advice charities. Research planned with proxies, carers, and people without digital access.
📊
Independently verifiable public value
Every outcome is measured against government-published statistics — ONS Excess Winter Deaths, DWP benefit take-up rates, DfE EYFS Profile, HMCTS tribunal statistics. The Theory of Change is built to be verifiable, not just credible.
🔬
Evidence-grounded in formal research
Perry Preschool (early years ROI). Heckman Equation. MOJ Legal Problem and Resolution Survey. National Energy Action Fuel Poverty Monitor. ADR UK impact evidence. The tools are built on — not instead of — the formal research in each domain.

The mentoring exchange

Mutual development, not one-way instruction

This programme is explicitly designed to develop skills — in me and in everyone who participates. I can offer specific things, and I'm looking for specific things in return. Mentoring relationships here are genuine exchanges.

What I can offer you

  • The whole-programme perspective — product, policy, engineering, and research on a live public sector AI programme simultaneously
  • Navigation of the DSIT/GDS/NDL/Blueprint landscape from the inside — what's real, what's noise, what matters
  • Practical AI engineering skills being developed in real-time — MCP, TypeScript, GOV.UK APIs, Claude Code — shared as I learn them
  • A genuine stake in a programme that could reach millions of people in some of the hardest circumstances
  • Access to the networks being built — ADR UK, GOV.UK Chat, co-design community organisations, partner departments
  • Direct honest feedback on work, career strategy and positioning — not just encouragement

What I'm looking for

  • Senior digital and AI leaders in government who've built things that shipped — and know what got left on the floor
  • Experienced TypeScript and MCP engineers who can accelerate the technical learning curve by reviewing code and spotting patterns
  • Community organisers and advocates who can connect the programme to communities with lived experience — as genuine partners, not consultees
  • Academics in adjacent research fields — public administration, AI ethics, health informatics, access to justice, early years — who want a policy delivery channel for their findings
  • People who've navigated NDL, ADR UK, or UKRI research partnerships and can help me avoid the wrong doors
  • Anyone who'll challenge the assumptions in this work with evidence and without ego
For junior and mid-career colleagues: there is space here to take real ownership of a domain, a tool, or a research strand. This is not shadowing — it is doing. The learning curve is steep but the support is real and the work is meaningful. If you're looking for a portfolio project with genuine public value, reach out.
